Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return winds the Salmon River. The Salmon was given the nickname long ago as the River of No Return by the people living near it because of the number of folks that came to visit and fell in love with its rugged beauty.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through canyons in central Utah, the Green River flows towards the Colorado River. High grey stone cliffs, desert scenery, and great rapids make this run famous. This 84-mile trip offers outstanding scenery, evidence of prehistoric and historic human activity, and thrilling whitewater.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is the granddaddy of all river trips. It combines world-class whitewater with breathtaking scenery, ancient ruins, and side canyons only accessible by river. Rapids are rated on a unique scale from I to X due to the variety of river conditions.
